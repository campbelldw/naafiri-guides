Why play her top at all

VERIFIED At Emerald+ she's a solid A-tier top (52.2% WR, rank ~20/110, 13.3k games). At your elo she is lolalytics' #1 graded top laner — S+, 52.18%, 13.9k Gold games. Only 6% of Naafiri games happen top, so nobody knows the matchup. The lane is longer, the enemies are melee juggernauts who can't punish 900-range Q poke, and bush control is packmate heaven. The catch: it's a kill lane with no safety net — Teleport measurably loses on her (below).

Bruiser top? The honest data

VERIFIED Eclipse-first is picked in 21% of top games and wins only 49.7% — the lethality core beats it top just like mid. The real bruiser-adjacent line top is late-slot: Death's Dance 56.8%, Maw 58.1%, Shojin 56.4%, Endless Hunger 61.0%, Bastionbreaker 63.1% (n=236). So: lethality items 1–3, bruiser items 4–5. The Eclipse → Cleaver → Shojin full-bruiser line exists (~1/7th of the play) but has no statistical edge. And the split-push fantasy is dead: Hullbreaker top has n=3. Your Setup D (mid guide) transfers as written for the vs-bulky-comp games; expect the same measured burst tax.

4 · Top-specific win condition

REASONING from the verified pieces: top Naafiri's game is lane kill → plates → Herald window → group mid as the extra assassin nobody drafted against. You are not a side-lane 1-3-1 piece (no TP, no Hullbreaker data, falls off vs stacked armor) — after two items, treat top like mid's §7 loop: crash the side wave, then be at every mid-map fight with R up.
